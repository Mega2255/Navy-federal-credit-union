Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Navy Federal

Navy Federal Credit Union is a federally chartered credit union — not a bank. Credit unions are member-owned financial cooperatives, meaning profits go back to members in the form of lower rates, fewer fees, and better services. We are federally insured by the NCUA up to $250,000 per depositor.
Yes. Navy Federal uses military-grade 256-bit encryption for all data in transit and at rest. Your accounts are protected by biometric authentication, real-time fraud monitoring, and instant card freeze controls. All deposits are federally insured by the NCUA up to $250,000.
We protect your money and data through multiple layers of security: end-to-end encryption, two-factor authentication, real-time transaction alerts, and 24/7 fraud detection. Your personal data is never sold to third parties, and our privacy policy strictly limits how information is shared.
Yes. Navy Federal provides unique IBANs (International Bank Account Numbers) to all eligible members. Your IBAN enables you to send and receive international wire transfers seamlessly. You can find your IBAN in the app under Account Details, or by contacting member support.
To open a Navy Federal account you'll need: a valid government-issued photo ID, your Social Security Number (SSN) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), proof of military affiliation or eligibility, and a minimum opening deposit (which may be waived for eligible members). The entire process takes about 10 minutes in our app.
Opening a Navy Federal checking or savings account is completely free. There are no application fees, no monthly maintenance fees, and no minimum balance requirements. Some premium account tiers may have optional paid features, but our standard accounts cost nothing to open or maintain.
You can switch to Navy Federal from virtually any bank or financial institution — including Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citibank, USAA, and more. Our Switch Kit guides you through transferring your direct deposits, recurring payments, and account balances step by step, with no disruption to your finances.
Risk level indicates the likelihood that you may lose some or all of the money you invest in a financial product. Savings accounts and checking accounts carry zero investment risk and are NCUA-insured. Investment products like stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds carry varying levels of market risk, and their value can go up or down.
